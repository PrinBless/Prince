Traditional photography

A traditional photo refers to a photograph that is captured using conventional methods, typically involving film-based cameras and darkroom processes for development. This type of photography emphasizes classic techniques, such as natural lighting, posed compositions, and staged settings. It contrasts with modern digital photography, which uses digital sensors and editing software. Traditional photos often have a timeless, nostalgic quality due to their slower, more deliberate creation process.

Candid photography

Candid photography captures moments naturally, without posed expressions or planned setups. The subject is often unaware of the camera, resulting in genuine emotions and spontaneous reactions. It focuses on storytelling and real-life moments like laughter, tears, or surprise. This style is widely used in weddings, events, and street photography. Candid photos are artistic and full of life, often taken from creative angles. They help preserve raw, emotional memories as they truly happened.
